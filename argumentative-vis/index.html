Analysis of Survival Factor in Tintanic Incident

The Sinking of the RMS Titanic is the most famous ship disaster in the North Atlantic between the night of April 14 and the early morning of April 15, 1912, five days after its maiden voyage from Southampton, England, to New York Harbor, the world's largest cruise ship at the time. At 2340 hours on Sunday, 14 April 1912, six sea ice warnings had been received before the collision with an iceberg, but the ship was approaching its maximum speed when the lookout spotted it. Unable to turn quickly, the ship suffered an impact on its starboard side, causing a gap in parts of the hull and flooding five of its 16 watertight compartments. The Titanic was designed to withstand only four watertight compartments, and sank.s the passengers were put into the lifeboats, they used distress bombs and radiotelegrams to call for help. According to shipping industry practice at the time, the Titanic's lifeboat system was designed to "ferry" passengers to other nearby ships, rather than to "evacuate" all personnel to take refuge in lifeboats at the same time, and was therefore far from sufficient in number; With the ship sinking quickly, many passengers and crew were unable to board lifeboats hours before other vessels arrived. To make matters worse, poor evacuation management has led to many lifeboats being launched before they are fully full. Two hours and 40 minutes later, the ship sank.
